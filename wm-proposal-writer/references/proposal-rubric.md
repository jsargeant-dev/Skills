# Proposal And Pitch Narrative Rubric

Use this as a revision checklist before final output.

## 1. Strategic Clarity

Question: Is the case obvious?

Strong draft signs:

- the core message is clear fast
- the main ask is obvious
- the “so what” is unmistakable

Weak draft signs:

- buried lead
- vague value
- overly technical framing

## 2. Audience Alignment

Question: Is this built for them?

Strong draft signs:

- reflects client priorities, risks, and KPIs
- uses the client’s language
- anticipates objections

Weak draft signs:

- sounds reusable for any client
- talks mostly about us

## 3. Compelling Case For Change

Question: Why change now?

Strong draft signs:

- states the stakes and cost of inaction
- makes the status quo uncomfortable
- shows urgency

Weak draft signs:

- describes a solution before proving need
- makes change feel optional

## 4. Differentiation And Positioning

Question: Why West Monroe?

Strong draft signs:

- includes specific, credible differentiators
- ties our approach to this client’s situation
- makes alternatives feel incomplete or risky

Weak draft signs:

- “deep expertise”
- “best in class”
- generic capabilities language

## 5. Logical Flow And Narrative Arc

Question: Does it tell a story?

Target arc:

- Client Problem
- Insight
- Stakes
- Solution
- Why West Monroe
- Proof

Weak draft signs:

- section order feels list-like
- no buildup or momentum

## 6. Proof And Credibility

Question: Can the client believe it?

Strong draft signs:

- relevant examples
- quantified impact where supported
- claims backed by evidence

Weak draft signs:

- bold claims without proof
- proof not aligned to the proposed work

## 7. Executive Presence

Question: Does it sound confident?

Strong draft signs:

- direct language
- clear recommendations
- no hedging

Weak draft signs:

- defensive or uncertain tone
- passive phrasing
