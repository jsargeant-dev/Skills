# West Monroe Brand Voice Summary

Use these rules as hard constraints.

## Voice

- Lead with the point.
- Use clear, direct, plain language.
- Focus on outcomes and action.
- Sound like a capable partner working alongside the reader.
- Balance business and technology.
- Keep momentum with active voice and concise sentences.

## Do

- Be pragmatic and outcome-focused.
- Emphasize collaboration and shared ownership.
- Be decisive without hype.
- Use standard American English and the Oxford comma.

## Do not

- Use em dashes.
- Use buzzwords, filler, or stacked adjectives.
- Sound abstract, promotional, or jargon-heavy.
- Make unsupported claims.
- Talk down to the reader.

## Messaging priorities

- Applied insight over theory
- Business and technology together
- Collaboration over distance
- Measurable impact over vague future value

## Approved messaging use

- Brand narrative: conceptual reference only
- Elevator pitch: adaptable starting point
- Boilerplate: only when a formal company description is required verbatim
